Contractor FAQ

Overview

Self aware panel

EASWORK talent is another term for “freelancer.” EASWORKS may interchangeably use the terms “freelance,” “EAS talent,” or “Expert” or “contractor” or “consultant “ to describe the members of our EAS Talent Network. An Expert is an individual that is skilled in one or more of our work categories. Experts perform specific tasks related to work assignments for specific client. EAS Talent work with the Customer to agree upon weekly or monthly deliverables and set expectations. EAS talent contracts generally track their time hourly for each client and work closely with EASWORKS Customer success manager.

 How do I join EASWORKS as a contractor?

Fill out the application at EASWORKS.com/join. Once we have reviewed your application, Based on opportunity, we hand-select the most appropriate candidates for each EAS project opportunity according to their professional skills, experience, domain expertise and availability. Once you have been invited to a enterprise application role, you will receive a link that allows you to apply for the position.

 How does EASWORKS helps Enterprise Application Talent like you?

EASWORKS is platform for Enterprise Application Tech Talent and employers and its primary goal is to find you an Enterprise Application job you’ll love.

In addition to this goal, we have resources to help you get there such as helpful articles in the EASWORKS blog on interview tips as well as technical assessments to help you prove your skills, and showcase your domain expertise and scores to employers who will interview you (and make job offers), at a higher rate.

 If I complete my profile, how will EASWORKS use it?

First, a complete and approved profile is required in order to be selected for any upcoming opportunities. That means that if you're looking for an EAS job opportunity any time soon, it's a good idea to complete it in advance. Once complete, our Technical team will reach out to schedule a profile approval meeting.

 How do I apply for a position?

Once you’ve completed your profile & approved on our platform and mark your status as "Actively looking for new jobs", we will actively promote your profile to clients and your eligible to Apply for EAS job opportunities which will be shared to you.

We use your profile as a universal job application to save, As soon as we have found any opportunity that fits your EAS skills, we will contact you to see if you are interested. The vetting process begins after you have applied for the role by answering a set of questions specific to the position along with work skill assessment.

Who is a Customer Success Manager?

Our Customer Success Managers (CSM) work as the main point of contact for both Customers and EAS Talent. CSMs are core team of EASWORKS who work with EAS Talent and Customers to ensure projects, Customers, and EAS Talent are successful.

Can I contact EASWORKS clients directly?

Here are some guidelines to be followed during different engagement phase cycle:

When applying for New job role. All communication with clients is handled by EASWORKS until you're selected to interview with the client.

After an interview When you sign on as a EASWORKS talent, you agree that you won't contact EASWORKS clients outside of the platform until you begin working together. We ask that you don't message them on any social platforms.

While working on an engagementOnce a contract is in place, clients will determine how they'd like the work to unfold. You'll be put in touch directly with the client, and you'll be using one of the project collaboration tools preferably SLACK and you would align to that specific project cadence .

After an engagementBefore working with you on the EASWORKS platform, clients sign a two-year non-solicitation agreement. If they're interested in working with you again after the initial engagement has ended, they'll reach out via EASWORKS, and we'll spin another engagement back up if you're interested and available. You're totally welcome to keep a professional relationship with your past EASWORKS clients, knowing that they've committed to a two-year non-solicitation
