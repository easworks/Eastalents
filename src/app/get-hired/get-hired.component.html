Work skill Assessments (WSA)

A Work skill Assessment (WSA) or Behavioural assessment is a tool that's designed to observe, understand, explain and predict a person’s behavior which is different from a personality assessment.

The WSA takes as little as 7 minutes to complete and determines how individuals see themselves responding to workplace situations that they perceive to be favourable or challenging, and reveals whether their response patterns are active or passive.

Giving insight based on widely recognized & proven DISC theory (Dominance, Compliance, Influence and Steadiness) the WSA will generate WSA results in real time so that the recruiting manager can confidently analyze the candidate’s behavior and determine quickly if this is a suitable candidate for the role being applied for.

It’s really an assessment and not a test .We use assessments to learn about a perspective or way of thinking. It has no right or wrong answers. You cannot pass or fail the assessment. Answering the questions only indicates a person's view or perspective. There is no way to get it wrong.

Work skill Assessment (WSA) or Behavioural assessments help organizations and recruiting managers understand the level of interpersonal skills that a person has and help predict how they might respond to new and different scenarios.

A person will be hired for having the right skills, knowledge and technical expertise but are most often overlooked from cultural fitment of organization they lack the appropriate interpersonal skills.

Work skill Assessment (WSA) or Behavioural assessments help ensure that the best-fitting candidates are hired and that the right people are assigned for specific roles. They enable people to increase their self-awareness and help individuals and groups to better communicate with each other.

Based on DISC theory, a behavioural assessment will analyze a person’s fears, motivators, values and behavioural style using four main profile factors: Dominance, Influence, Steadiness and Compliance.

The EASWORKS Behaviour assessment is our behavioural assessment tool that takes these profile factors into account and tests candidates' attitudes in each area. Plotted on a graph, these four areas will be analysed by proprietary technology that gives employers and recruiters insight into a candidate’s behavioural style and helping the recruitment process move in the right direction.
